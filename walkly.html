Walkly

Project Video

Created for hackCMU 2017. Android application that encourages users to exercise by tracking their steps and pledging money to local organizations. Designed and implemented all frontend components, including UI/UX.

Team: Manini Amin, Pallavi Bannai, Arushi Patel, Saloni Saxena

Role
Frontend | UX

Ideation

Our team wanted to create an Android application that tracked a user's steps and allowed them to set monetary and physical goals. A user could set a rate for each step and once the goal was reached, a user could donate the money raised to a local charity.

We started by listing out the features that we wanted to include in our application. The features that we felt were essential were written on a orange post-it note and those less crucial on a pink. This allowed us to map out what we wanted our app to encompass.

From there, I started to sketch out a storyboard for how a user would interact with the application. From the Home Screen a user can click 'Start' to access the progress for their current goal. The number of steps they have taken so far would be displayed, along with the selected rate and the amount of money raised so far for the given charity. From this page, a user can either select 'History' or 'New Goal'. The History page would allow a user to see and edit their past goals and achievements. The New Goal page would allow a user to set a new goal, rate, and charity through a list generated by a charities API.

Iteration

Having only a little bit over a day to complete our submission, there unfortunately wasn't much time to go through several iterations of the design. I created wireframes of each of the screens based on my initial storyboards. The biggest change from the first iteration to the last was the Home Screen. The Home Screen represented our application and we wanted it to be visually appealing and inviting. We messed around with the name, typography, color palette, and icons. From there, I made some tiny changes in each screen to better fit the backend design.

Final Touches

Reflections

As this was our first hackathon, our team learned a lot from the experience. For one, it was hard for us to work swiftly and not spend too much time fixing the little things that didn't affect the overall purpose of the app. In terms of the design process, we also couldn't spend much time researching or iterating through our design, as the frontend came second to the backend. In terms of technology, we came out with proficient knowledge of Android Studio, Sketch, and Java.
